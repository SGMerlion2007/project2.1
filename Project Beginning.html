The Beginning of the Jumbo Jet

The A380 was conceived when Boeing had been dominating the ultra-high-capacity airliner (UHCA) market with its 747. There were several key factors that prompted Airbus to enter the UHCA market. Firstly, Airbus needed a jumbo to complete their own range of aircraft. Another reason why the A380 was conceived was because they wanted to break the dominance Boeing had in the jumbo jet industry. In addition, there were many other Boeing competitors who tried to break the UHCA market. For example, companies such as Lockheed and McDonnell Douglas had attempted to rival the 747, but the projects either failed or were still being deliberated, which gave Airbus the perfect time to strike.

The A380 was initially announced in June 1994 under the name A3XX. However, on the 19th of December 2000, the board of Airbus voted to launch a €9.5 billion ($10.7 billion) project to build the A3XX, which was to be re-designated as A380. Subsequently, the aircraft configuration was finished in 2001, and manufacturing of the first A380 component began in January 2002. By the time the first A380 was delivered, the development cost had grown to €11 to 14 billion.
